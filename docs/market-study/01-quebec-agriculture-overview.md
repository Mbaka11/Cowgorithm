# Quebec Agriculture Overview

> This document provides a foundational overview of the Quebec agriculture landscape to inform all four Cowgorithm market segments. Data is primarily sourced from the 2021 Census of Agriculture (Statistics Canada) and the Canadian Dairy Information Centre (CDIC).

---

## 1. Quebec's Position in Canadian Agriculture

Quebec is one of the two pillars of Canadian agriculture alongside Ontario. The province's diverse farming sector and strong cooperative traditions make it a significant contributor to the national agricultural economy.

### Farm Count & National Share

| Metric                      | Quebec (2021) | Canada (2021) | QC Share |
| --------------------------- | ------------- | ------------- | -------- |
| Total farms                 | 29,380        | 189,874       | 15.5%    |
| Farm operators              | 42,265        | 262,455       | 16.1%    |
| Change in farms (2016→2021) | +1.6% (+461)  | −1.9%         | —        |

**Notable**: Quebec's farm count _increased_ while the national total declined. Quebec's share rose from 14.9% to 15.5%.

_Source: Statistics Canada, Census of Agriculture, 2021 (Release: 2022-06-15)_

### Key Agricultural Commodities (Quebec's Share of Canada)

| Commodity         | QC Share of Canada (2021) | Change from 2016 |
| ----------------- | ------------------------- | ---------------- |
| Maple taps        | 89.1%                     | −1.4 pp          |
| Cranberry acreage | 58.4%                     | +3.9 pp          |
| Blueberry acreage | 40.9%                     | +4.3 pp          |
| Dairy cows        | 36.4%                     | −0.6 pp          |
| Pigs              | 29.6%                     | −2.4 pp          |

Together, Ontario and Quebec accounted for:

- **40.9%** of total farms in Canada
- **69.8%** of Canada's dairy cows
- **57.6%** of Canada's hogs and pigs

_Source: Statistics Canada, Census of Agriculture, 2021, Quebec Provincial Profile (96-325-X)_

### Economic Scale

- Quebec's biofood sector (agriculture, food processing, fisheries, retail) contributes approximately **$30 billion** to the provincial economy annually <!-- VERIFY: MAPAQ Profil sectoriel -->
- Maple syrup exports alone: **$568.9 million** in 2021
- Quebec accounts for **91.3%** of maple syrup farms in Canada

_Source: Statistics Canada, Census of Agriculture, 2021, Quebec Provincial Profile_

### Quebec: Leader in Robotic Milking

Quebec's dairy sector is demonstrably tech-forward. The province accounts for **41.1% of all robotic milking farms in Canada**, and the number of farms using robotic milking nearly **doubled from 454 to 902** between 2015 and 2020.

This indicates:

- Dairy farmers in Quebec are **willing to invest** in automation technology
- There is an existing culture of **precision dairy management**
- These farms already generate data and would benefit from additional sensor/GPS layers

### Precision Agriculture Adoption (Broader)

While specific QC precision-ag adoption rates are not fully broken down in the Census, industry estimates suggest:

- **GPS guidance / auto-steer on tractors**: ~20–30% of larger crop farms <!-- VERIFY: precision agriculture survey data -->
- **Herd management software**: Widespread on dairy farms using robotic milking; estimated ~40–50% of commercial dairy farms use some form of digital herd management
- **Automated feeding systems**: Growing alongside robotic milking adoption
- **Drones and sensors**: Emerging, mostly on larger crop operations

### Telecommunications Infrastructure

Rural connectivity is a key enabler for IoT devices like GPS collars:

- **Bell** and **Telus** both offer LTE-M and NB-IoT networks in Quebec
- **Operation High Speed** (federal-provincial): $1.2B program to bring high-speed internet to 98% of Canadian homes by 2026 <!-- VERIFY: current status for rural QC -->
- Rural cellular coverage has improved significantly but gaps remain in remote regions (Abitibi-Témiscamingue, Côte-Nord, northern QC)
- The primary dairy belt (Montérégie, Centre-du-Québec, Chaudière-Appalaches) has **good LTE-M coverage** from major carriers

## 4. Government Programs Supporting Ag-Tech

### Federal Programs

| Program                                                  | Description                                                                      | Relevance to Cowgorithm                |
| -------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **NRC-IRAP**                                             | Up to ~$1M in advisory and funding for innovative tech SMEs                      | Core R&D and prototyping funding       |
| **Sustainable Canadian Agricultural Partnership (SCAP)** | Federal-provincial cost-share for on-farm technology adoption (successor to CAP) | Farmer subsidy for adopting Cowgorithm |
| **Farm Credit Canada (FCC)**                             | Loans and financing for farm equipment and technology                            | Farmers can finance collar hardware    |
| **CanExport Innovation**                                 | Funding for R&D partnerships with international entities                         | Potential for tech partnerships        |
| **SR&ED Tax Credits**                                    | 15% federal refundable credit for R&D (35% for eligible CCPCs)                   | Ongoing R&D cost offset                |

### Quebec Provincial Programs

| Program                                                      | Description                                                                                             | Relevance to Cowgorithm                                                               |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Innov'Action agroalimentaire** (MAPAQ)                     | Multi-year program supporting innovation in agri-food; grants for technology adoption and modernization | Primary QC funding source for both Cowgorithm (as supplier) and farmers (as adopters) |
| **Programme d'aide à la transition numérique**               | Supports digital tools adoption on farms                                                                | Farmer-side subsidy for digital ag tools                                              |
| **SODEC / Investissement Québec**                            | Provincial investment and tax credits for tech startups                                                 | General startup funding                                                               |
| **PME 2.0 / Audit numérique**                                | Digital assessment and transformation for SMEs                                                          | Business process digitization                                                         |
| **Crédit d'impôt pour la recherche scientifique (QC SR&ED)** | Provincial R&D tax credit (~14% refundable) stacks on federal SR&ED                                     | Stacks with federal credit for ~49% total R&D credit                                  |

### Key Considerations

- The **combined federal + provincial SR&ED credits** can offset nearly half of R&D spending — critical for a bootstrapped startup
- **SCAP cost-share programs** can subsidize farmer adoption (potentially 50–75% of technology costs), reducing the barrier to purchasing Cowgorithm collars
- **IRAP** is the single most important early-stage program — provides both funding and advisory services
- Many programs require the product to have Canadian-based R&D and manufacturing → aligns with Cowgorithm's Quebec base

## 5. Farmer Pain Points

### Aging Workforce & Succession Crisis

| Metric                     | Quebec (2021) | Canada (2021) |
| -------------------------- | :-----------: | :-----------: |
| Average farm operator age  |  54.0 years   |       —       |
| Operators under 35         |     8.6%      |     8.6%      |
| Operators 35–54            |     38.0%     |     30.9%     |
| Operators 55+              |     53.4%     |     60.5%     |
| Farms with succession plan |     8.5%      |     12.0%     |
| Female operators           |     27.7%     |     30.4%     |

_Source: Statistics Canada, Census of Agriculture, 2021_

**Key insights**:

- **Over half of QC operators are 55+** and trending older (up from 47.3% in 2016)
- Only **8.5% have a succession plan** — below national average — meaning many farms face uncertain futures
- Young operators (<35) are a declining share at just 8.6%
- Growing off-farm employment: **45.8% of operators work off the farm** (up from 36.3% in 2015)

**Implication for Cowgorithm**: Labor shortage and aging workforce create strong demand for automation and monitoring tools that reduce the need for physical observation. A GPS collar with virtual fencing can replace the labor of checking fences and locating cattle.

### Labor Shortage

- Quebec agriculture faces chronic labor shortages, especially for manual tasks (fencing, herding, pasture rotation)
- Increasing reliance on Temporary Foreign Workers (TFW) program
- Rising minimum wage and competition from other sectors make farm labor expensive
- **Automation is not optional — it's necessary for farm survival**

### Rising Operational Costs

- Feed costs, fuel, and input prices have risen significantly post-2020
- The expenses-to-revenues ratio is tightening margins even for supply-managed sectors
- Farmers need technology that demonstrates clear **ROI through cost reduction** (less time on fencing, better pasture utilization, reduced animal losses)

### Environmental & Regulatory Pressure

- Quebec has **strict environmental regulations** for animal production, including manure management and methane reduction targets
- Phosphorus loading limits affect livestock density per hectare
- The federal government has set targets for **methane reduction from agriculture**
- Precision management of grazing through virtual fencing can help optimize land use and reduce environmental impact — a selling point for Cowgorithm

### Supply Management Constraints

- Entry into dairy/poultry is extremely expensive due to quota costs
- Existing quota holders are well-capitalized but cautious with new expenses
- Any new technology must demonstrate clear value within the supply-managed framework
- Positive angle: supply management provides **income stability**, meaning farmers can plan for recurring subscription costs
